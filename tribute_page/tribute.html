Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

The Man Who Fought For India's Freedom

Here's a time line of Mahatma Ghandi's Life:

  • 1869 - Born and raised in a Hindu Family in Gujarat, Western India
  • 1880 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the Universit
  • 1915 -He set about organising peasants, farmers, and urban labourers to protest against excessive land-tax and discrimination.
  • 1921 - Gandhi led nationwide campaigns for easing poverty, expanding women's rights, building religious and ethnic amity, ending untouchability, and above all for achieving Swaraj or self-rule.
  • 1930 - Gandhi led Indians in challenging the British-imposed salt tax with the 400 km (250 mi) Dandi Salt March
  • 1940 - Gandhi's vision of an independent India based on religious pluralism was challenged in the early 1940s by a new Muslim nationalism which was demanding a separate Muslim homeland carved out of India.
  • 1942 - led the quit India movement
  • 1947 - Britain granted independence, but the British Indian Empire was partitioned into two dominions, a Hindu-majority India and Muslim-majority Pakistan
  • 1948 - He was assasinated at the age of 79. Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist, who assassinated Gandhi on 30 January 1948 by firing three bullets into his chest.
